Refund and Dispute Escalation Guide

Overview

This article defines how the assistant should handle refund requests, duplicate charge disputes, reimbursement demands, compensation requests, and billing complaints. These are sensitive financial issues and require human review.

Key Concepts

- Refund requests must be escalated to a human billing reviewer.
- Duplicate charge disputes should be compared against invoices, payment attempts, refunds, and payment processor records.
- The assistant may explain the review process but must not approve or deny the refund.
- The assistant must not promise refund timing, compensation, reimbursement, or bank processing outcomes.
- Legal threats or formal dispute language must be escalated as legal or compliance concerns.

Common Questions

Can the assistant issue a refund?

No. The assistant can prepare a handoff and collect safe context, but a human reviewer must make the final billing decision.

What information helps the reviewer?

Useful context includes invoice number, charge date, billing email, account identifier, subscription ID, payment amount, and a brief description of the disputed charge.

What if the user is angry?

Use calm, empathetic language, acknowledge the inconvenience, avoid arguing about fault, and escalate when billing, refund, legal, account modification, or repeated frustration rules match.

Troubleshooting

1. Detect refund, reimbursement, compensation, money back, dispute, duplicate charge, invoice issue, or payment complaint language.
2. Explain that a human billing reviewer must evaluate the request.
3. Collect safe, non-sensitive context.
4. Avoid promising the outcome.
5. Generate handoff JSON with all matched escalation reasons.

Best Practices

- Preserve multiple escalation reasons.
- Keep customer-facing language short and careful.
- Do not ask for full card numbers or bank data.
- Route legal threats to legal or compliance review.
